Research Question

To what extent does the initial seed mass (Big, Medium, Small) influence the germination rate and early growth of mangrove seeds when subjected to varying light intensities (30%, 50%, 75% shade)?

Aim: To determine if larger seeds have enough nutrient reserves to compensate for low light levels compared to smaller seeds.

Bi-Weekly Measurement Protocol — Every 2nd Thursday
1
Prepare all measurement tools: ruler/calipers, scale (optional), camera, and this dashboard open on a device.
2
Water all 4 plots first (3L salt + 7L fresh water), check salinity with FG-212 refractometer before and after mixing.
3
Measure stem length (cm) of ALL 120 seeds. Work group by group — Control → 30% → 50% → 75%. Enter each into the Bi-Weekly Log tab.
4
Count leaves on all seeds that have sprouted. Record germination status for each seed (Not germinated / Sprouting / Germinated / Dead).
5
Weigh seeds on a scale if possible (optional but recommended for the research data).
6
📷 Photograph each of the 4 grid plots from above. Label photos with session number and group name.
7
After logging all 120 seeds, click ⬇ Export JSON in the top bar and upload the file to the shared team Google Drive folder.
8
The next team member to open the dashboard imports the JSON using ⬆ Import JSON to stay in sync.
